Louisian Gumbo

Louisiana Gumbo

The Southern Secret Sauce

So, your looking to make a southern claissic? Well you've come to the right place! This down south delight will warm you up during the winter and fill bellies all year round! Starting off with a flour base, (rue), cooking down your rue is the first step and your main compnent to a good ol fashion gumbo. season if you like or use the flavors from all the meats that will be added later. Cooking your rue is simple put 1/4 cup of perferred oil in your pot and heat, add 2-3 table spoons (or as needed) of flour to the pot and whiske until golden brown or to disired darkness. Next is the bulk of the southern concoction, THE MEAT with a mixture of flavors the skys the limit!! from chicken and Sausage to a full seafood smorgasbord!! gumbo has everything in it but the kitchen sink!! in a seperate pot (or the same pot as the rue, just take the rue out first) throw your meats in and brown until tender or when you thtink its ready haha. AS you find your meats being done, your rue ready to go, grab a 5 qt pot, (or the biggest pot you have haha)and fill half way with water. once your waters boiling throw in you rue and stir until fully combined witht eh water it should should be a dark brown and smelling like heaven!! After you have a good boil thrown in your meats and let that boil for 1.5-3 hours. yes we know thats a long time but its well worth the wait!! As that boils all the sweet nothings combind in the pot and make an amazing mixture your taste buds will tahnk you for later! serve over a bed of rice in a bowl and pressto!!

Ingredients!

  • cooking oil
  • flour
  • oinion powder
  • Garlic powder
  • chopped bell pepper
  • minced garlic
  • green oinions
  • 1 lbs of chicken (bone-in or out)
  • 1 lbs of sausage(perferrably cajun
